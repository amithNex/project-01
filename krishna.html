Why krishna ?

Krishna is not just a deity; he is a profound symbol of love, wisdom, and the ultimate truth in Hindu philosophy. Here are a few reasons why Krishna holds such a special place

Krishna’s relationship with Radha epitomizes the soul’s longing for union with the divine. Their love transcends physicality and speaks of an eternal, spiritual bond

Krishna, as the charioteer in the Mahabharata, teaches Arjuna (and humanity) the essence of life, duty, and righteousness through the Bhagavad Gita. His wisdom is timeless and continues to guide millions.

Known for his leelas (divine play), Krishna teaches that life is a blend of duty and joy. His mischief as a child and his charm as a young man show us to embrace life fully.

Krishna is seen as a protector of the weak and a true friend to those in need. His help to Draupadi during her humiliation and his loyalty to Sudama are legendary.

Krishna's form and teachings represent the infinite—blending love, power, and wisdom. He is both the cosmic creator and the personal friend, making him relatable yet divine.

Krishna invites us to see life as a joyful journey of connection with the divine while fulfilling our earthly roles. Why do you find Krishna intriguing?

Important Characters

Radha

Radha is Krishna's eternal consort and a symbol of love, devotion, and divine connection.

Balram

Krishna’s elder brother, known for his strength and loyalty, and often depicted with a plow.

Yashoda

The foster mother of Krishna, known for her unconditional love and care for young Krishna.

Arjuna

The Pandava prince and Krishna’s friend, to whom Krishna imparted the wisdom of the Bhagavad Gita.

Nanda Maharaj

The chief of Gokul and a loving father figure who protected Krishna during his childhood.

Kansa

A tyrannical king who tried to kill Krishna, fearing a prophecy of his own death at Krishna’s hands.

Devaki

Imprisoned by her brother Kansa, she gave birth to Krishna in a miraculous way.

Vasudeva

Smuggled baby Krishna to safety across the Yamuna river under divine guidance.

Draupadi

Krishna protected her during the infamous disrobing incident, showcasing his role as a divine savior.

Rukmini

Represents marital love and devotion. Her elopement with Krishna is a famous story of love triumphing over obstacles.

Krishna's Talks

योगस्थः कुरु कर्माणि संगं त्यक्त्वा धनंजय।

(Yogasthah kuru karmaani sangam tyaktvaa dhananjaya.)

"Be steadfast in yoga, O Arjuna, perform your duty without attachment."

कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन।

(Karmanye vadhikaraste ma phaleshu kadachana.)

"You have the right to perform your duty but not to its fruits."

सुखदुःखे समे कृत्वा लाभालाभौ जयाजयौ।

(Sukha-duhkhe same kritva labhalabhau jayajayau.)

"Treat pleasure and pain, gain and loss, victory and defeat as the same."

समः शत्रौ च मित्रे च तथा मानापमानयोः।

(Samah shatrau cha mitre cha tatha manapamanayoh.)

"One who is equal towards friend and foe, and in honor and dishonor."

Festivals of Krishna

Janmashtami

Celebrates Krishna's divine birth with joy, fasting, and midnight prayers.

Raas Leela

A vibrant celebration of Krishna’s divine dance with Radha and the Gopis.

Govardhan Puja

Marks Krishna lifting Govardhan Hill to protect the villagers of Vrindavan.

Holi

Known as the Festival of Colors, it celebrates Krishna’s playful spirit.
